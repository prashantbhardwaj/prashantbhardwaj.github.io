1. Glasgow to Oban

  • Start in Glasgow and head west on the M8 towards Greenock.
  • Take the exit onto the A82, following signs for Crianlarich and Loch Lomond.
  • Enjoy the scenic drive along the shores of Loch Lomond, one of Scotland’s most stunning lochs.
  • Continue on the A82, passing through charming villages and picturesque landscapes as you make your way north.
  • After approximately 47 miles, you’ll reach the village of Tyndrum. Consider taking a break and exploring the nearby attractions, such as the beautiful waterfalls of the Falls of Falloch.
  • From Tyndrum, continue on the A85 towards Connel and Oban.
  • As you approach Oban, you’ll be treated to breathtaking views of the coastline and the islands of the Inner Hebrides.
  • Arrive in Oban, a bustling town known as the “Gateway to the Isles.” Explore the charming harbor, visit the iconic McCaig’s Tower for panoramic views, and perhaps even take a ferry to explore the surrounding islands.

The route from Glasgow to Oban offers a blend of stunning scenery, including Loch Lomond, picturesque villages, and coastal vistas. It’s a journey that showcases the natural beauty of Scotland’s west coast and provides an opportunity to experience the charm of Oban.

So, embark on this memorable road trip, enjoy the panoramic views, and immerse yourself in the coastal delights of Oban and its surrounding area.

2. Over the sea to Skye

  • Start your journey in the village of Kyle of Lochalsh, located on the mainland of Scotland.
  • Cross the iconic Skye Bridge, which connects the mainland to the Isle of Skye. Enjoy the scenic drive across the bridge, with panoramic views of the surrounding sea and mountains.
  • As you enter the Isle of Skye, you’ll be greeted by breathtaking landscapes, dramatic cliffs, and picturesque villages.
  • Follow the A87, the main road on Skye, and prepare to be captivated by the island’s rugged beauty.
  • Pass through the village of Broadford, where you can make a stop to explore local shops and cafes.
  • Continue your journey north on the A87, taking in the stunning views of the Cuillin Mountains and the sparkling waters of Loch Ainort.
  • Arrive in the charming town of Portree, the largest settlement on Skye. Take some time to explore its colorful harbor, visit local shops and galleries, and perhaps enjoy some fresh seafood.
  • From Portree, you can further explore the island’s many attractions, including the Old Man of Storr, Fairy Pools, and the Quiraing.

The route “Over the sea to Skye” is a scenic and memorable journey that takes you from the mainland of Scotland to the enchanting Isle of Skye. With its breathtaking landscapes, charming villages, and natural wonders, Skye offers a true escape into the beauty of the Scottish Highlands.

So, embark on this unforgettable road trip, cross the Skye Bridge, and immerse yourself in the wild and awe-inspiring landscapes of the Isle of Skye.

Scotland‚Äôs Most Scenic Driving Routes

  • Most Scenic Routes

1. Angus Coastal Route - Dundee to Aberdeen

The Angus Coastal Route takes you along the stunning coastline from Dundee to Aberdeen. Enjoy scenic views of the North Sea, charming fishing villages, sandy beaches, and rugged cliffs. Along the way, you can visit attractions such as Dunnottar Castle, Montrose Basin Nature Reserve, and the picturesque town of Stonehaven. Indulge in delicious seafood, explore coastal trails, and soak in the natural beauty of the Angus coast.

2. Argyll coastal route Tarbert to Fort William

The Argyll Coastal Route takes you on a scenic journey from Tarbert to Fort William, showcasing the breathtaking beauty of the Argyll region. Travel along the picturesque coastline, pass by charming towns, and marvel at historic castles such as Inveraray Castle. Enjoy panoramic views of Loch Fyne, explore Glen Coe’s stunning landscapes, and immerse yourself in the rich culture and history of this coastal route.

3. Borders historic route Carlisle to Edinburgh

The Borders Historic Route takes you from Carlisle to Edinburgh, offering a journey through Scotland’s fascinating history. Explore ancient abbeys, magnificent castles like Hermitage Castle, and visit historic sites such as Abbotsford, the former home of Sir Walter Scott. Discover charming market towns, picturesque countryside, and experience the unique blend of Scottish and English influences in the Border region.

4. Clyde Valley tourist route Abington to Hamilton

The Clyde Valley Tourist Route runs from Abington to Hamilton, taking you through the scenic Clyde Valley. Explore the lush landscapes, rolling hills, and the picturesque River Clyde. Visit attractions like New Lanark World Heritage Site, Falls of Clyde, and enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and cycling along the route. Discover the rich heritage and natural beauty of the Clyde Valley.

5. Deeside tourist route Perth to Aberdeen

The Deeside Tourist Route stretches from Perth to Aberdeen, offering a delightful journey through the stunning scenery of Royal Deeside. Pass by picturesque towns, enchanting castles like Balmoral Castle, and enjoy the breathtaking landscapes of the Cairngorms National Park. Take part in outdoor activities, explore local heritage, and experience the charm of the Deeside region.

6. Fife coastal route Kincardine to Newport

The Fife Coastal Route takes you along the beautiful coastline of Fife, from Kincardine to Newport. Enjoy panoramic sea views, visit charming fishing villages like Anstruther and Pittenweem, and explore historic sites such as St Andrews Cathedral and Castle. Experience the world-famous golf courses, indulge in fresh seafood, and discover the rich maritime heritage of the Fife coast.

7. Forth Valley tourist route Edinburgh to Stirling

The Forth Valley Tourist Route takes you from Edinburgh to Stirling, offering a journey through the heart of Scotland’s historic heartland. Explore the iconic landmarks of Edinburgh, including the Edinburgh Castle and the Royal Mile. Continue to Stirling and visit Stirling Castle, the Wallace Monument, and the picturesque town of Falkirk. Experience the rich history, stunning landscapes, and cultural treasures along this route.

8. Galloway tourist route Gretna to Ayr

The Galloway Tourist Route runs from Gretna to Ayr, taking you through the scenic beauty of the Galloway Forest Park and the stunning coastline of Galloway. Enjoy the tranquility of nature, spot wildlife, and visit attractions like the Galloway Dark Sky Park, Drumlanrig Castle, and Culzean Castle. Explore charming towns, sample local delicacies, and immerse yourself in the natural wonders of Galloway.

9. Highland tourist route Aberdeen to Inverness

The Highland Tourist Route takes you from Aberdeen to Inverness, showcasing the scenic beauty of the Scottish Highlands. Travel through majestic mountains, pass by tranquil lochs, and explore historic sites such as Culloden Battlefield and Dunrobin Castle. Experience the untamed wilderness, visit charming Highland villages, and discover the rich Gaelic culture along this captivating route.

10. Moray Firth route Inverness to Loch Fleet

The Moray Firth Route runs from Inverness to Loch Fleet, offering a picturesque journey along the stunning Moray Firth coastline. Enjoy beautiful sandy beaches, spot dolphins and other marine wildlife, and explore charming fishing villages such as Nairn and Lossiemouth. Visit attractions like Cawdor Castle and the historic town of Dornoch, and experience the unique coastal landscapes and rich heritage of the Moray Firth.

11. North and west Highland route Ullapool to John o’Groats

The North and West Highland route is a scenic drive that takes you through the rugged and remote landscapes of the Scottish Highlands. Starting in the picturesque fishing village of Ullapool, you’ll embark on a journey to John o’Groats, the northeastern tip of the mainland.

As you drive along this route, you’ll be treated to breathtaking views of mountains, lochs, and dramatic coastlines. Pass through charming Highland towns and villages, including Lochinver, Durness, and Thurso. Marvel at iconic landmarks such as the stunning Eilean Donan Castle, the magnificent Ben Hope mountain, and the mystical Smoo Cave.

The route offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and exploration. Hike through the beautiful Assynt region, enjoy wildlife spotting at Handa Island Nature Reserve, or take a boat trip to spot dolphins and seals along the coast. Don’t miss the chance to sample delicious local seafood and traditional Highland cuisine at the charming eateries along the way.

Whether you’re seeking adventure, natural beauty, or a glimpse into Scotland’s rich history and culture, the North and West Highland route has it all. Prepare to be enchanted by the untamed beauty and captivating landscapes of the Scottish Highlands.

12. North Coast 500 North coast

The North Coast 500 is a legendary route that takes you on a thrilling adventure around the scenic North Coast of Scotland. Drive through rugged landscapes, pass by stunning castles, and explore charming coastal towns. Marvel at the dramatic cliffs of Cape Wrath, visit the mystical Smoo Cave, and enjoy breathtaking views of iconic landmarks such as the Old Man of Storr and the Dunrobin Castle. Prepare to be captivated by the beauty and grandeur of the North Coast.

13. Perthshire tourist route Greenloaning to Ballinluig

The Perthshire Tourist Route takes you through the heart of Scotland’s picturesque Perthshire region. Travel from Greenloaning to Ballinluig, passing by lush countryside, historic sites, and charming villages. Discover the beauty of Loch Tay, explore the enchanting Hermitage woodland, and visit iconic attractions like Blair Castle and Scone Palace. Immerse yourself in the stunning landscapes and rich heritage of Perthshire.

14. The Snow Roads scenic route Blairgowrie to Grantown-on-Spey

The Snow Roads Scenic Route is a breathtaking journey through the Cairngorms National Park, taking you from Blairgowrie to Grantown-on-Spey. Experience the beauty of the Scottish Highlands as you drive through mountain passes, past sparkling lochs, and alongside dramatic landscapes. Admire the grandeur of Balmoral Castle, visit the picturesque town of Braemar, and soak in the serenity of the Cairngorms. Prepare to be mesmerized by the captivating scenery along the Snow Roads.

15. The South West Coastal 300 South west

The South West Coastal 300 is a spectacular route that showcases the natural beauty and cultural heritage of South West Scotland. Drive along the coastline, pass through charming villages, and discover hidden gems along the way. Enjoy panoramic views of the Solway Firth, visit historical sites like Culzean Castle and Robert Burns Birthplace Museum, and explore the unspoiled landscapes of Galloway Forest Park. Prepare to be enchanted by the diverse attractions and stunning vistas of the South West Coastal 300.
